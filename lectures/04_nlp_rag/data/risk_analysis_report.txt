"comprehensive risk analysis" for essential entities.

5 Conclusion and Recommendations 
The assessment identified five High-level risks—three cyber and two physical—that 
could disrupt electricity delivery or violate regulatory obligations. Immediate focus 
should be on: 
1. Hardening remote-access paths (VPN zero-day exposure, R-02). 
2. Closing unnecessary field-device ports to prevent external Modbus 
reachability (R-03). 
3. Accelerating the warm-standby data-centre build to mitigate single-site 
dependency (R-05). 
Full execution of the treatment plan will lower the overall residual risk profile from 
High to Medium and align the organisation with NIS2 Article 21 expectations for 
"appropriate and proportionate technical and organisational measures." 
Appendix A – Methodology 
The analysis follows ISO 27005:2022 risk steps: context establishment → 
identification → analysis → evaluation → treatment → monitoring. 
Appendix B – Review Schedule 
This report is reviewed annually or after any major system change or incident with 
CVSS ≥ 8 impact.
